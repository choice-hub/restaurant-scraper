> Source: internal sales handbook (June 2026). For pricing details see 03_pricing.md.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Trial & Payments

**Can I try it before committing?**
Best approach is a personalized demo profile where the client can try receiving orders, managing reservations, and customizing their website. As a last resort, a 1-month free trial can be offered. If integrations are needed, the Setup Fee still applies.

**How does payment work?**
Pricing depends on the plan scope. Payments can be monthly, 6-monthly, annual, or 2-year. A one-time Setup Fee is paid at the start of the cooperation.

**Where do the funds from online payments go?**
Payments go through the Adyen payment gateway directly to the restaurant's bank account.

**How quickly do I receive order funds?**
Within 48 hours.

**Why is the QR payment fee lower than delivery?**
QR payments are in-house transactions — simpler, fewer steps, lower risk. Delivery involves more steps (order, preparation, courier handoff, delivery), possible integrations (Wolt Drive split), and higher chargeback risk — hence slightly higher fees.

### Support & Account Management

**Who manages my account after signing?**
After signing and paying, the After Sales team handles the launch and onboarding. A Customer Success Manager then maintains regular contact. Customer support is available **7 days a week**.

**Can I use Choice with my existing systems?**
Yes — compatible with Storyous, Dotykačka, and other POS systems listed on the website.

### Features & Flexibility

**Can orders come directly to the POS without the Choice Business app?**
Yes for QR table orders — they can go directly to the POS. For delivery and takeaway, the Choice Business app is recommended for order management and prep time settings.

**Can I set different prices on the ordering system vs. delivery marketplaces?**
Yes — the menu editor supports different pricing per channel.

### Hardware & Setup

**What hardware is needed? Can I rent it?**
Recommended: iPad or Lenovo tablet. Clients source hardware themselves, or Choice can supply a Lenovo tablet for 4,000 CZK / ~€160.

**How long does setup take after payment?**
The contract allows 21 days, but in practice setup is usually completed much sooner.

### Marketing & Data

**How do customers find out about direct ordering?**
Choice helps with promotion via QR codes, social media integration, and marketing tools.

**Can I use customer data for my own marketing?**
Yes — the system collects contacts for marketing purposes in accordance with GDPR.

### Company & Positioning

**What is Choice?**
Choice is a Czech-origin company founded during the pandemic, now operating in 25+ countries with 25,000+ businesses.

**What's the difference between Choice and Qerko?**
Choice is a comprehensive restaurant platform — beyond QR payments it includes ordering system, reservation system, delivery integrations, and more. Both use MIF++ payment scheme, but Choice's fee is lower. Key difference: with Choice, all customer data (names, contacts, order history) fully belong to the restaurant. With Qerko, restaurants get only limited data (first name + last payment time) — the full database stays with Qerko.

**Am I locked into a long contract?**
Not necessarily — monthly contracts are available. Longer commitments get better pricing (12mo −10%, 24mo −20%).

**Does Choice work in Slovakia / euros?**
Yes — operates in both CZ and SK; prices and payments handled in EUR too.

**Do I need to be on Wolt to do delivery?**
No. Via **Wolt Drive**, you can use Wolt couriers without being on their marketplace — own delivery without own couriers.

## Common Objections & How to Handle Them

### "It's working fine the way it is."
Ask about details to find hidden pain points. When they mention something occasionally not working, offer a mini demo: *"That's exactly what we solve — let me show you in a minute."*

### "We have an IT person / we do it ourselves."
Respect it and frame Choice as complementary: *"We work with IT, not against them — we cover the operational side, orders and marketing."*

### "We want to keep the personal touch."
System saves time so staff can talk more with guests, not less. *"QR doesn't remove contact — it just speeds up service."*

### "Our customers are older / won't use QR."
Share concrete examples from similar venues. Offer a test at one table without commitment.

### "We're a small place, this is for bigger restaurants."
*"Exactly — small places save the most time. Every person counts."* Especially effective when they mention staffing issues.

### "We tried it before and it didn't work."
Ask what specifically failed. Then address that specific issue: *"That's exactly what we do differently — we set everything up for you."*

### "It's expensive / another cost."
Switch from cost to ROI: *"It only makes sense if it pays back — let me show you the calculation."* Never defend price with an immediate discount.

### "We don't want anyone taking a cut."
*"We're not a marketplace — you pay for the system, not a % of turnover."*

### "We have another system."
Show integration, not replacement: *"We integrate with most POS systems, so nothing changes."*

### "Paper menu is enough."
*"You can keep the paper menu — QR just complements it and saves reprinting every time you update."*

### "Paper reservation book is enough."
*"Customers want to book anytime — even after closing. The online system handles that without errors."*

### "Marketplaces are enough."
*"Marketplaces are great for starting, but over time they take 20–30% of every order."*

### "Instagram is enough, I don't need a website."
*"Instagram attracts attention; a website drives action."* When they get many DMs: *"A website saves them typing and saves you time."*

### "No time to implement it."
*"That's why we set everything up — it won't interrupt your operations."*

### "Staff won't manage it."
Show proof: *"We have restaurants where 60+ year old staff use it daily."* Offer in-person training.

### Qerko-specific objections (June 2026)
- **"Qerko works, we don't want to change QR payments"** → *"We do QR payments too — in most cases with a lower fee. Send me your monthly statement and I'll calculate the exact saving."*
- **"We're locked in a Qerko contract"** → Explore if the contract actually locks the device specifically; some clients switched without penalty.
- **"Qerko mirrors menu from POS, you don't"** → Acknowledge, redirect to overall value (fee savings, own data, full platform). POS menu mirror is in development — don't promise a timeline.
- **"Qerko gave us better conditions / a discount"** → *"Discounts are usually time-limited. Let me calculate the real numbers from your actual figures."*

## General Objection Method
1. **Don't go defensive** — first understand: *"What exactly do you mean?"*
2. **Confirm and reframe** — acknowledge the feeling, pivot to value
3. **Proof** — reference client, numbers, ROI calculation, mini demo
4. **Move forward** — concrete next step (test, savings calculation, signing)

**Rule: never trash competitors, never promise what isn't confirmed.**
