## What Is ICP for Choice?

ICP = a venue where we can create **real economic value**.

Typically has:
- Sufficient **customer volume**
- **Operational pain** that we solve
- **Open mindset**
- **Ambition to grow**

ICP is NOT about:
- City size
- Type of cuisine
- Age of the restaurant

It's about the combination of: **volume + pain + mindset**

### Ideal Client Mindset
**They say things like:**
- "How much does this cost us today?"
- "How can we improve this?"
- "If it works, we'll expand it."
- "I want to own my data."

## ICP by Product — Detailed

### Own Delivery
**ICP criteria:** 50+ orders/month, or 5,000+ Instagram followers, or 400 CZK+ average order on marketplaces
**Typical venues:** sushi, burger, pizza, casual dining, modern bistro
**Current setup:** own website, marketplace (Wolt/Bolt/Foodora), or both
**Key pain:** high marketplace commissions, low margin, no customer control, expensive custom development, customers belong to the marketplace

**Discovery questions:**
- How many orders do you have per day? (→ calculate monthly)
- How many delivery vs. takeaway?
- What is your average basket value?
- What commission are you currently paying to marketplaces?
- Where do most of your orders come from?
- Do you have your own orders or only marketplace?
- Do customers come back? Do you have a customer database?

---

### Collection Point
**ICP criteria:** 150+ marketplace orders/month from 2+ channels
**Current setup:** multiple tablets, multiple apps, multiple admin panels, manual re-entry to POS
**Key pain:** bar chaos, menu management in multiple admins, no POS integration, errors in order-taking

**Discovery questions:**
- How many tablets do you have at the counter?
- Which platforms do you use?
- How many orders come in per day?
- Does staff re-enter orders manually into the POS?
- How many admin panels do you use to update menus?

---

### QR Orders & Payments
**ICP criteria:** 10+ tables, strong peak periods
**Current setup:** classic table service, integrated POS, or competitor QR solution
**Key pain:** high staff costs, slow service, waiting, low table turnover, high competitor fees

**Discovery questions:**
- How many tables do you have?
- How many waiters are on the floor during peak?
- How long does a guest typically wait before ordering?
- Do guests sometimes wait to pay?
- What % commission do you pay on QR payments?
- Do you have an IC++ model?

---

### Reservation System
**ICP criteria:** using competitor system OR taking reservations manually/by phone/via form to email
**Current setup:** phone, Instagram, competitor system
**Key pain:** no-shows, reservations across multiple channels, no guest database, not connected to other digital tools

**Discovery questions:**
- How many reservations do you have per week?
- How do you currently take reservations?
- Do you use any system?
- How often does a guest not show up?
- How do you use your guest database?

---

### All-Products / New Venue
**ICP criteria:** new venue OR using 2+ competitor systems
**Key pain:** chaos between systems, no integration, wants to consolidate everything

**Discovery questions:**
- What systems do you currently use?
- How many different tools are in operation?
- Are you planning any technology changes?

## Anti-ICP

These venues are typically NOT a good fit:
- Old-style pubs with no growth ambition
- School canteens
- Corporate cafeterias
- Bakeries without seating
- Bubble tea / donut stands
- Pure retail
- Venues without online orders

---

## Quick ICP Filter

If **2+ of these apply**, the deal is risky:
- ❌ Weak volume
- ❌ Zero operational pain
- ❌ Closed mindset ("it's working, don't change anything")
- ❌ Technology lock-in (POS that doesn't integrate)
- ❌ Unsuitable concept (Anti-ICP type)

## When NOT to Recommend the Product

A good salesperson doesn't just sell — they also **recognize when the product isn't the right solution**.

If during discovery we find:
- The venue doesn't meet ICP criteria
- The client has no real pain
- Our product doesn't actually solve their problem

…it's completely fine to professionally end the meeting and explain this openly.

**Goal:** not to sell at any cost, but to build a **win-win partnership** where:
- The client gets real value
- We gain a satisfied long-term customer

Selling where it doesn't make sense risks: dissatisfied client, high churn, unnecessary support load.

**How to say it:**
- "Based on what you've described, I'm not sure our solution would bring you enough value right now."
- "I think at this moment our system probably wouldn't have the effect it should. I wouldn't want to sell you something that wouldn't really help."
- "Based on what we've discussed today, I feel our product may not be ideal for your type of operation. If the situation changes, I'd be happy to revisit."
